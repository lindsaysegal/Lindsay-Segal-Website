Data Analysis Between Interent Useage And Happiness

This study is aimed to contribute to a deeper understanding of the relationship between internet use, happiness and overall well being. Motivation for this research comes from the growing significance of technology use in many aspects of people's lives today and seeing how future policies can be made in order to minimize the gap between those who do have access to technology and the internet and those who do not. An analysis using charts and graphs made using R and Tableau were used to see a relationship between internet use and happiness across many countries with additional variables including perceived social support, and health life expectancy were used to further the understanding of the relationship between internet use and happiness. The research shows that there is a correlation between internet use rate and happiness scores. Findings emphasized regional variations between this relationship. Socially, there was an overall correlation between internet use rate and perceived social support. This could suggest that increased internet use contributes to social connectedness and could also increase happiness. Overall, these findings can be used to acknowledge that there is a gap in internet usage between countries and that it does affect happiness. Future policy can help minimize the divide and provide technology to those who lack it.
